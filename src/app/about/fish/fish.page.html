What is Salmon?

Salmon is both caught wild and farmed (and maybe even domesticated by some).

Atlantic Salmon are typically farmed, and Pacific Salmon are usually caught in the wild.

 The Life of Wild Salmon

Early Years

Salmon hatch from eggs at the bottom of a stream or lake.

They spend years 0-4 in freshwater eating insects and crustaceans, and avoid predators like birds and larger fish.

A Young Stud

After 4 years or so, the salmon swim downstream to the deep blue sea.

Their scales change to a shiny silver color, teeth grow in, and the Salmon grow in length.

For the next 1-7 years they start eating plankton, squid, and other fish and bulk up a ton.

The Epic Migration

Now the Salmon has found its calling.

These big, strong Salmon begin the journey upstream, all the way back to their birthplace in freshwater.

They stop at nothing to make it home. Not only swimming against the current, but literally flinging themselves through waterfalls!

Most Salmon do not eat anything during this journey, and it shows - the ones that make it all the way home look near-dead.

The Females dig little depressions in the lake (redds) and lay eggs, to which the Males proceed to fertilize before both fish parents die shortly after.

 Basic Recipe

Start with raw, thawed, & cooled fish

Cut into cooking-size pieces

Cut 3-4 slits halfway down through the top of the salmon

Season top with salt, olive oil

Season bottom skin with pepper, olive oil

Put frying oil in pan and get it hot

Start salmon skin-down and fry until 3/4 of the side is light pink

Flip & cook the top for a few minutes

Get the sides cooked & crispy and add any extra seasons like basil
